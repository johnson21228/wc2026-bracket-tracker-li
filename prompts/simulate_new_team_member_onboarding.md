# Simulate a New Team Member Onboarding to This Workbench

You are simulating a smart new team member who has just been invited to this Workbench repo with read-only access.

They are not trying to edit code yet. They are trying to understand what the Workbench is, why it exists, and how they would participate responsibly.

Use the uploaded repo pack as the only source of truth.

## Role

Act as a newcomer reading the repo for the first time.

## Tasks

1. Explain what this Workbench appears to be.
2. Explain what problem it is trying to solve.
3. Explain how the Workbench Loop works.
4. Explain what “Capture Back” means.
5. Identify the first 5 files the newcomer should read.
6. Identify what the newcomer should not touch or assume yet.
7. Identify what would be confusing to a newcomer.
8. Identify what would make the newcomer comfortable participating.
9. Suggest a first low-risk action the newcomer could take.
10. Suggest 5 questions the newcomer should ask the Workbench owner before contributing.

## Output style

Be candid and newcomer-oriented.

Do not give a full technical audit.

Do not suggest broad refactors.

Focus on whether the repo successfully onboards a new collaborator into the Workbench workflow.
