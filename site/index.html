How Bracketeering Hub works

Bracketeering Hub is being developed as one continuous two-game World Cup pool. Group Stage asks players to predict the Round of 32 field before the final group-stage matches are played. Knockout Stage resets the bracket to the official FIFA-supplied Round of 32 field, then asks players to pick winners through the knockout rounds.

When the site is live for real play, the transition from Group Stage to Knockout Stage should feel seamless: Group Stage closes at the Group Stage Finale, then Knockout Stage opens on the official FIFA Round of 32 bracket.

Development preview

The Dev Stage selector is present while the games are still being developed. It lets viewers preview Group Stage and a simulated Knockout Stage before the site is opened for real pool play.

For now, viewers are encouraged to make Group Stage picks and try the simulated Knockout Stage view.

Group Stage Rules:

Before the final round of group-stage matches begins, choose the teams you think will make the Round of 32.

Group Stage locks at the start of the Group Stage Finale: all Group Stage picks must be submitted before kickoff of the earliest third-round group-stage match.

Some Round-of-32 places may already be known by then. That is part of the game. Many other places will still depend on final group-stage match results, including standings, goal difference, and third-place qualification.

Knockout Stage Preview

Knockout Stage is the knockout-bracket game. It starts from the official FIFA-supplied Round of 32 field, not from each player’s Group Stage predictions. During development, Knockout Stage may use simulated or fallback Round of 32 assignments so the bracket flow can be tested.

Scoring preview

  • Group Stage: 1 point for each correctly predicted Round-of-32 team.
  • Knockout Stage preview scoring: later knockout rounds are expected to be worth more than earlier rounds.

Rule status

These rules are provisional while Bracketeering Hub is under development. Before real pool play begins, the pool owner should lock the final scoring rules and game timing.
