How to play

This is your World Cup Bracketeering Hub. Tap or click each bracket slot and pick a team. This is Bracketeering!

Navigate the game board like Google Maps: scroll, zoom, and pan to move around, then tap or click to make your picks.

If a pick turns red, open that cell and choose a currently available team to correct it.

Your picks are saved in your browser for now. This website may later evolve to store your picks online and let players view other players’ picks.

The game has two parts

Group Stage asks players to predict the teams that will reach the Round of 32 before the final group-stage matches are played.

Knockout Stage starts from the official Round of 32 field and asks players to pick winners through the knockout rounds.

Group Stage

Group Stage picks are locked at the start of the Group Stage Finale. All Group Stage picks must be submitted before kickoff of the earliest third-round group-stage match.

Group Stage is used as the first tiebreaker if there is a tie after the final match on July 19.

Some Round-of-32 places may already be known by the time Group Stage locks. That is part of the game. Other places will still depend on final group-stage results, including standings, goal difference, and third-place qualification.

Knockout Stage

Knockout Stage picks are locked when the first knockout match begins.

Scoring

Group Stage: 1 point for each correctly predicted Round-of-32 team. The team must be in the correct bracket slot.

Knockout Stage:

  • 1 point for each correct Round of 32 winner.
  • 2 points for each correct Round of 16 winner.
  • 4 points for each correct Quarterfinal winner.
  • 8 points for each correct Semifinal winner.
  • 16 points for correctly picking the World Cup champion.

Knockout points are awarded when the team you picked wins that match. Later rounds are worth more because they are harder and more important.
